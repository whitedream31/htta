Our Town Twinning Association

What is Haverhill & District Town Twining Association?

Our friendly association aims to promote friendship and understanding between different European countries.

We have events all around the year that supports what we do.

About Haverhill Town

Haverhill is a market town and civil parish in the county of Suffolk, England, next to the borders of Essex and Cambridgeshire. It is about 14 miles southeast of Cambridge, and 47 miles northeast of central London.

Haverhill is the second-largest town in the Borough of St Edmundsbury and has a population of around 30,000 people.

Haverhill is twinned with two towns, Pont-Saint-Esprit in France, and Ehringshausen in Germany.

Districts and Villages

Haverhill has many villages that surround it. The association includes these villages. Many of our members live in them.

Kedington

Kedington is situated in the county of Suffolk. It is approximately 16 miles southeast of Cambridge and about 14 miles northeast of Haverhill.

It has a history that dates back many centuries, since medieval times. The village is surrounded by the picturesque Suffolk countryside, with rural landscapes, farms, and woodlands nearby.

We often have events at the Kedington Community Centre

Hundon

Hundon is approximately 20 miles south of Bury St Edmund's and about 12 miles north of Haverhill.

Like many villages in England, Hundon has a history that dates back many centuries, with historical features and buildings in and around the village.

Being a village, Hundon has a close-knit community, and has local amenities such as a village hall, All Saints Church, the Rose & Crown pub, and even has its own football club.

The village is surrounded by the scenic Suffolk countryside, with rural landscapes, farms, and woodlands nearby.

We often have events at the village hall.
